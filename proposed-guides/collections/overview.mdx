---
title: "Collections overview"
description: "How money comes into your Fincra wallet, how to reconcile it, and how to handle refunds and chargebacks."
---

Collections are inbound payments that credit your Fincra wallet. This page explains the
model and the API surface for tracking, reconciling, and disputing them. Anyone
receiving money starts here.

<Note>
  Collections are **created upstream** — a payer sends money to a virtual (reserved)
  account, a pay-with-transfer flow, or a mobile-money instruction set up through the
  relevant product. This service does not expose a "create a charge" endpoint; you
  observe collections as they arrive and act on them.
</Note>

## The model

<Steps>
  <Step title="A payer sends money">Into a virtual account or via a supported inbound rail tied to your business.</Step>
  <Step title="Fincra credits your wallet and fires a webhook">You receive a `collection.successful` event (verify its signature).</Step>
  <Step title="You reconcile">Look the collection up by reference, merchant reference, or channel reference and match it to your order.</Step>
  <Step title="You act if needed">Initiate a refund, or accept/reject a chargeback.</Step>
</Steps>

## Reconcile a collection

Match incoming money to your records. Look up by whichever reference you hold. See the
reference: [`GET /reference/{reference}`](/api-reference/openapi/fincra-collections#tag/payments/get/reference/{reference}).

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="cURL">
    ```bash
    curl "https://sandboxapi.fincra.com/collections/reference/COL-9f81a2c3" \
      -H "api-key: $FINCRA_SECRET_KEY"
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Node.js">
    ```javascript
    const res = await fetch(
      "https://sandboxapi.fincra.com/collections/reference/COL-9f81a2c3",
      { headers: { "api-key": process.env.FINCRA_SECRET_KEY } },
    );
    const collection = await res.json();
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Python">
    ```python
    import os, requests
    res = requests.get(
        "https://sandboxapi.fincra.com/collections/reference/COL-9f81a2c3",
        headers={"api-key": os.environ["FINCRA_SECRET_KEY"]},
    )
    collection = res.json()
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

You can also fetch by your own reference
(`/merchant-reference/{merchantReference}`), by numeric id (`/{id}`), or by the
provider's reference (`/channel-reference`). Lists are paginated (`page`, `perPage`).

## Refunds

Return a collection to the payer. This is asynchronous; the result arrives via a
`refund.*` webhook. See [`POST /refund`](/api-reference/openapi/fincra-collections#tag/refunds/post/refund).

<Warning>
  A refund moves money. Send a unique reference and make retries idempotent — do not
  re-issue a refund on a timeout without first checking its status.
</Warning>

## Chargebacks

When a payer disputes a card collection, a chargeback is raised. You respond through the
API: list them, then accept (concede) or reject (contest with evidence). See
[`PATCH /chargebacks/{id}/accept`](/api-reference/openapi/fincra-collections#tag/chargebacks/patch/chargebacks/{id}/accept)
and `/reject`.

## Additional-information requests

Compliance may raise an additional-information request on a collection (AML follow-up).
List open requests on a collection and submit your response via
`PATCH /{id}/additional-information`.

## Test it in sandbox

Trigger an inbound bank transfer without real money using the simulate endpoint, then
watch the webhook and reconcile.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Simulate an inbound transfer">`POST /collections/transfer/simulate` with a sandbox amount and your business id.</Step>
  <Step title="Receive the webhook">Expect `collection.successful`; verify its signature.</Step>
  <Step title="Reconcile">Fetch the collection by reference and confirm the amount and status.</Step>
</Steps>
